Why We Exist

A real story from the other side of the table.

"Most companies that buy houses see a transaction. I see the family — because I was the family."

When I was younger, my family went through foreclosure. I watched my parents try everything to save our home. As a kid, I tried to learn the process myself — too late. We lost the house.

That experience never left me. I learned firsthand how confusing, isolating, and unfair the process can feel when you are on the wrong side of it. And I learned that most "we buy houses" companies show up too late, with too little empathy, treating you like a transaction instead of a person.

I started Local Home Solutions to be different. Family-owned, locally rooted, and built around one promise: I will listen first, treat you with respect, and only offer a solution if it actually helps you. If it does not, I will tell you that too — and point you toward something that might.

If you are in a tough spot, you are not alone. Let us just talk first. There is no obligation, and no pressure ever.

Common Questions

Honest answers to what most sellers want to know.

We have been asked everything. Here are the questions that come up most often — and clear answers. If yours is not here, just ask.

How quickly can you close on my house?

As fast as 7 days, or as long as you need. You set the closing date — not us. If you need 30, 60, or 90 days to find your next place or settle affairs, that is fine. We work on your timeline.

Are there any fees, commissions, or closing costs I have to pay?

None. Zero fees, zero commissions, and we cover all standard closing costs. The number we offer is the number you walk away with — no surprises at the closing table.

Do I need to clean out the house or make repairs?

No. We buy houses completely as-is, in any condition. Take what you want and leave the rest — old furniture, junk in the basement, anything. We handle the cleanout. No painting, no staging, no inspections.

I am behind on my mortgage or facing foreclosure. Can you still help?

Yes — this is one of the most common situations we help with. The earlier you reach out, the more options you will have. Even if you have already received foreclosure notices, there is often still time to sell and protect your credit.

I inherited a property in Chicago but I do not live there. Can you handle this remotely?

Absolutely. Many of our sellers are out-of-state heirs dealing with probate or an inherited home they cannot manage from a distance. We coordinate with local title companies, handle paperwork digitally, and can close without you ever flying in.

How do you calculate your offer?

We base it on three things: comparable sales in your neighborhood, the property's current condition, and what it'll cost us to repair and resell. We will walk you through the math line by line so you can see exactly how we got there.

What if I have tenants in the property who will not leave?

We can buy the property with tenants in place. You do not need to handle eviction, deal with non-paying renters, or wait for a lease to end.

Why would I sell to you instead of listing with a real estate agent?

Honestly, sometimes you should not. If your house is in good condition and you have time to wait, listing with an agent often nets you more money. We are the right choice when speed, certainty, or simplicity matters more than top dollar.

Is there any obligation if I request an offer?

None. The conversation is free, the offer is free, and you can walk away at any point. If our offer does not make sense, we will often point you toward an option that does — even if it is not us.
